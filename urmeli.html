Sybyzgy

Sybyzgy is a Kazakh wind musical instrument. It is made of copper, wood, and sometimes brass. The length is 600-650 mm or 700-800 mm; 3-4 grooves. The sound range is diatonic, the volume is 2 1/2 octaves. Among the wind instruments, the flute is considered one of the most popular instruments for the people. It has become an integral part of folk music.

Sazsyrnai is a wind instrument. The bagpipe belongs to the group of whistled flutes. Some cymbals do not have a whistle mechanism. The Kazakh people are a good example of it. Instruments similar to sazsirnai are found in many nations. The shape of the instrument, whose name is pronounced in the language of each nation, is also different. Masters made its shape like a bird, a fish, a multi-headed horse, and an egg. The sound range of the sazsyrnai is a third, sometimes an octave. In 1971, during the excavations in Otyrar, a musical instrument like a goose egg made of clay was found. This sazsirna, which has been researched by instrumentalists, produces three sounds.
