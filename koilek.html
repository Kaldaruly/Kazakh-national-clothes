Kamzol

Kamzol (kamzol) is a type of women's national clothing, which is worn over the shirt. The collar and sleeves are embossed, the waist is narrow, and the hem is below the knee. It is sewn from precious, high-quality fabrics such as velvet, cotton, and silk. Kamzol is also called "sleeveless beshpent" or "kazekei" in some places. The vest does not have sleeves or a collar, and both regions consist only of the outside and the lining. Satin, khaki, and cotton are often used for lining of kamzols.

Shapan

Shapan is an ancient and traditional Kazakh garment. It is the national costume of Kazakhstan and the peoples of Central Asia. It is sewn from a thick fabric with wool and cotton in between, lined or double lined. It mainly has a straight collar, and partly has a folded collar. Cloak: it is divided into a sliding cloak, a side cloak, and a cut cloak.
